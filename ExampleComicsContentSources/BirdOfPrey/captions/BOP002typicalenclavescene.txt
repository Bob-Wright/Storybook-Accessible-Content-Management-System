After the great collapse, or as some called it, the &#34;Reset&#34;, had left society in disorder, large numbers of the survivors were held in what were euphemistically called &#34;Enclaves&#34;. After the collapse no one used the term &#34;refugee camp&#34; anymore, these were after all simply internment centers. Enclaves was a more palatable expression. People frequently left the questionable safety of the enclaves in an erstwhile yet almost invariably futile quest for some nebulous concept they called &#34;freedom&#34;.
